## 1. Show Title

## 5. Episode Naming Convention

### Recommended Template

```
Ep. [##] — [Situation or Topic] in Thai: [Specific Focus] ([Level])
```

**Design rationale:**
- `Ep. ##` — numeric ordering aids navigation and creates a sense of progression; numbered episodes perform better in browsing views than purely descriptive titles.
- `[Situation or Topic] in Thai` — embeds "in Thai" into every episode title, which feeds keyword density at the episode level and signals the language clearly in search.
- `[Specific Focus]` — the colon-clause makes the episode scannable; a listener can read just the colon-clause and know exactly what vocabulary they'll get.
- `([Level])` — level tag (Beginner / Intermediate) helps returning listeners self-sort and signals the audience to new listeners.

### Level Tag Conventions

| Tag | Use when |
|---|---|
| `(Beginner)` | Core vocabulary, common situations, no assumed prior knowledge |
| `(Intermediate)` | Builds on earlier episodes; more nuanced expressions or social contexts |
| `(All Levels)` | Recap, bonus, or culture episodes accessible to everyone |

### Three Example Episode Titles

```
Ep. 01 — Greetings in Thai: 10 Words for Meeting People (Beginner)
Ep. 07 — Ordering Street Food in Thai: Market Vocabulary (Beginner)
Ep. 14 — Getting Around in Thai: Taxis, Tuks, and Directions (Intermediate)
```

### Alternative Template (for consideration)

If the CMO prefers a warmer, walk-branded feel over SEO-forward structure:

```
Walk [##]: 10 Thai Words for [Situation]
```

Examples:
```
Walk 01: 10 Thai Words for Meeting Someone New
Walk 07: 10 Thai Words for the Night Market
Walk 14: 10 Thai Words for Getting Around Bangkok
```

**Recommendation:** Use the primary template (`Ep. ##`) for directory discoverability. The "Walk" variant is better suited to social media clips and marketing where brand identity matters more than search indexing.

## 6. Category Selections

### Apple Podcasts

Apple Podcasts allows one **primary category** and one **secondary category**, each with optional subcategories. Subcategory selection places the show in both the parent and child browse lists.

| Priority | Category | Subcategory | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Education | Language Learning | Exact-match category; directly where Thai learners browse. Language Learning is the highest-intent browse destination on the platform for this audience. |
| **Secondary** | Society & Culture | Places & Travel | Captures the expat, tourist, and heritage-learner audience who browse travel content. Also surfaces Walk Talk Thai alongside destination podcasts, creating a discovery path for trip-planning listeners. |

**Note on "Kids & Family > Education for Kids":** Do not select. Walk Talk Thai is adult-positioned content; the wrong category will suppress visibility with the target audience.

---

### Spotify

Spotify uses a simpler category system drawn from the RSS `<itunes:category>` tag (the same feed powers both platforms). It does not currently offer a standalone "Language Learning" top-level category in all markets, but Education is the correct parent.

| Priority | Category | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Education | Broadest-reach correct category; Spotify's Education browse tab is well-trafficked. |
| **Secondary** | Society & Culture | Same reasoning as Apple Podcasts; cross-discovery with travel and culture listeners. |

**Practical note:** Set these in your podcast host's RSS settings (category 1 and category 2 fields). Spotify and Apple Podcasts both read from `<itunes:category>` tags in the RSS feed, so a single correct RSS configuration covers both platforms simultaneously — and Pocket Casts by extension.

---

### Pocket Casts

Pocket Casts does not have a separate submission process or independent category system. It indexes from the same RSS feed. Correct Apple Podcasts / Spotify RSS categories will propagate to Pocket Casts automatically. No additional action required beyond a correctly tagged RSS feed.
